PML Sample Collection

Intro

During the benchmarking process of sample collection, vTMM uses a unique technique called PML. PML is a feature of Intel VT-x, which logs the guest physical address of dirty pages into a designated buffer. It's helpful to narrow down the page scanning range, comparing to the Linux's or Nimble's aimless page scanning.

But we do want to know if PML is really helpful in reducing overhead. The authors of vTMM refuesed to provide their source code. Here we are reimplementing this feature.

PML

PML is a mature machanism used by hypervisors to collect dirty page from guests. This is driven by the need for live VM migration. Because VMs' memory content are constantly changing, the VM has to be stopped before migrating when there is no PML support. PML make live migration possible by capturing those changed memory content, and fix the inconsistency by copying dirty pages again.

vTMM's strategy

vTMM implements sample collection in the hypervisor. vTMM's samples still come from page table scanning, but the scanning is hinted by PML. Only those pages logged by PML are scanned. (What's the point of scanning again? Logged by PML already means that address is dirty.)

To collect access count, vTMM scans 8 rounds (monitoring window/interval) at a time. Whether or not to clear the D bit depends on a MGLRU-like multi-level queue. Higer the level, hotter the page. Pages in the higher level also enjoy a do-not-disturb time of $2^{level-1}$ monitoring window. During DND time, the D bit will not be cleared. After the DND time ends, the D bit will be cleared. If the page is found again during the next monitoring window, it will be upgraded, otherwise, it will be downgraded.
